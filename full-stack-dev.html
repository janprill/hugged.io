Searching for a full stack dev with an entrepreneurial attitude

We are looking for full-stack developers (Typescript and Java) for a highly profitable, owner-managed company in Hamburg. Here you are a good fit as a generalist. From the technical concept to DevOps and coding, your team will make your project a success.

This is the place to be if you don't want to specialize in one thing only. You will find the advantages of a tech company without the disadvantages of the start-up culture.

Our client specializes in the integration of a leading Digital Asset Management (DAM) server. It also develops its own modules based on the DAM, in particular for the vertical retail market. These modules are used to implement sophisticated marketing solutions in the online/eCommerce and print sectors.

As a self-reliant developer, you build server components that are used in top 10 retail companies in Germany and the USA. Your websites fascinate a sophisticated audience that may be equipping its companies with designer furniture using your eCommerce solutions. The advertising material produced with your components is produced in runs of millions with the lowest possible personnel input.

Our client demands a lot from you. But it also gives you the freedom to learn all necessary skills and to keep your know-how up to date. You define realistic goals for your team and yourself.

Apply if you want to keep your brain on while optimizing your customers' processes so they can stay ahead with their DAM marketing and eCommerce solutions.

Don't apply if you want to be micromanaged don't want to utilize your mind.

Your challenges

  • Development of high-performance and fail-safe server components in Java 8.
  • Migration of monolithic components to microservices.
  • Great freedom in choosing your toolset to work in the JVM.
  • Customization of the UIs of the DAM in the frontend framework Angular with Typescript. Currently, AngularJS 1.x (latest) is used. Your tasks include porting to the latest version of Angular (currently Angular 5).
  • Your going to work in a team of ten developers.
  • You and your team are responsible for the entire stack of the solution. It is your success that you celebrate at the milestones and after the project is over.
  • Requirements engineering in DAM projects, often with a 7-digit budget.
  • Technical design of modules that are developed as individual developments for our customers because they go beyond the standard functions of the DAM server used.
  • Development of the designed modules in teams according to modern project management methods, which you have a decisive influence on.
  • Great freedom in the development and in the decision of architecture and development procedures. No micromanagement.
  • Take responsibility for project success with your team members.
  • Technical Project Management
  • DevOps by you, the developers.
  • A lot of freedom so that you can create your tools and optimize them.
  • Support of VMWare hosts.
  • Installation of Linux servers in the customer's data center or in the cloud.
  • Installation of a leading Java Digital Asset Management (DAM) server.

What makes working at our customer so great

  • Your project relies on you and your colleagues. You are not only responsible for individual project phases. It's literally for the whole thing. We love to create solutions. You will end up being proud to have made a project a success, not working through 10 unit tests and stories every day in a range of T-shirt sizes.
  • You and your colleagues are the company. You are important, both as a person and, of course, in an economic sense, and you are treated as such. We expect performance from each other and trust each other to make it possible.
  • You show what you can do and get what you need. We solve our customers' problems. We enjoy our performance. We bring us in a joint flow. The only political squabbling we want to see is at our competitors site.
  • Continuous learning: The team members are supported in teaching and coaching each other as part of their work.
  • Meaningful social rules: Our client has documented his expectation that his team will treat each other with respect without exception.
  • At least 3 month onboarding: We make a commitment when you join our client to help you learn key skills like our communication guidelines and how to combat imposter syndrome.
  • A smart, safe space: Our client has realized that better ideas and greater job satisfaction can be achieved if everyone feels they can contribute. He promotes broad cooperation accross the company.
  • Yes, yes, yes, yes, yes to well-equipped workplaces, fruit, room to relax, corporate events and a free Metrocard. Coffee and soft drinks are of course included. And what's so common, too. But what good does all this do you, if it only plants itself on an otherwise poisoned culture? With us, you have the opportunity to grow every day and to celebrate with your team the successes that you deserve.
